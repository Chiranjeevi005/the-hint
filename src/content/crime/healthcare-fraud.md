Federal prosecutors announced charges today against top executives of a major hospital chain, alleging a years-long scheme that defrauded Medicare and Medicaid of approximately $500 million.

## Alleged Scheme

The indictment describes:

- **Upcoding**: Billing for more expensive procedures than performed
- **Kickbacks**: Illegal payments to referring physicians
- **Medically Unnecessary Services**: Performing procedures patients didn't need
- **False Documentation**: Fabricated medical records to justify billing

## Corporate Accountability

The charges include conspiracy, healthcare fraud, and money laundering, with executives facing significant prison terms if convicted.
